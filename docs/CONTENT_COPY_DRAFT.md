## Problem

Healthcare infrastructure is moving toward greater automation, but autonomous medical systems require more than isolated devices or software dashboards. They need trusted orchestration layers that can coordinate distributed hardware, validate telemetry, monitor packet integrity, and preserve safety boundaries at every stage.

Vesperus is focused on that foundation: the control, validation, and supervision architecture needed before next-generation medical systems can become more autonomous, resilient, and scalable.

## NOVA Platform

NOVA is the current MVP and technical proof point for Vesperus.

It is a validated hardware/software orchestration platform designed around a safety-first architecture for distributed system supervision. The platform combines a dual ESP32-S3 architecture, Raspberry Pi gateway integration, the NOVA B1 motherboard, and the NOVA SC supervision console.

NOVA focuses on telemetry validation, packet integrity monitoring, structured command pathways, and system observability. Its purpose is to establish a credible orchestration layer for future medical automation research while maintaining clear safety boundaries.

NOVA is not presented as a clinically deployed system, a regulated medical device, or an enabled actuator-control product.

## M.E.D.I.S. Vision

M.E.D.I.S. is a long-term R&D application being developed on top of NOVA for resilient, decentralized healthcare infrastructure.

The vision behind M.E.D.I.S. is to explore how validated orchestration platforms could support future healthcare systems that are more distributed, robust, and responsive. By building on NOVA, M.E.D.I.S. remains grounded in a technical foundation focused on validation, telemetry integrity, and safety-first system design.

M.E.D.I.S. should be communicated as an R&D direction, not as completed medicine synthesis, clinical readiness, regulatory approval, patient deployment, or active autonomous treatment.

## Technology Stack

Vesperus brings together embedded systems, gateway computing, telemetry supervision, hardware validation, and software orchestration.

Core technical areas include:

- Dual ESP32-S3 embedded architecture
- Raspberry Pi gateway integration
- NOVA B1 motherboard design
- NOVA SC supervision console
- Telemetry validation
- Packet integrity monitoring
- Safety-first system architecture
- Future medical automation infrastructure

The technology stack is designed to support clear observability, controlled system boundaries, and reliable validation before higher-risk healthcare automation capabilities are introduced.

## Current Progress

Vesperus has established the foundational architecture for NOVA as the current validated platform and proof point.

Current progress includes hardware/software orchestration design, gateway-connected supervision, telemetry validation, packet integrity monitoring, and the definition of safety-first system boundaries.

The current phase is focused on credible infrastructure, validation, and readiness for future development. It does not claim clinical deployment, regulatory approval, enabled actuator control, or completed medicine synthesis.
