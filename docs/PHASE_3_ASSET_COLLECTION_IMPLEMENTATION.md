## Fallback Strategy

If required images are unavailable during website implementation:

- Use clean abstract CSS visuals instead of fake stock photos.
- Use structured cards, diagrams, and technical labels instead of misleading medical imagery.
- Use text-first credibility for Founder and Recognition sections.
- Use a text wordmark for Vesperus if no logo mark is available.
- Clearly label M.E.D.I.S. visuals as R&D concepts.
- Keep NOVA descriptions grounded in validated orchestration, telemetry validation, packet integrity monitoring, and safety-first architecture.

## Image Credibility Rules

- Use real screenshots, real photos, or real renders only.
- Do not use fake medical, hospital, doctor, patient, or clinical stock imagery.
- Do not use misleading device images.
- Do not use fake device renders.
- Do not imply clinical readiness, regulatory approval, patient deployment, enabled actuator control, active treatment, or completed medicine synthesis.
- Do not use Aivancity logos or publication assets unless permitted.
- Do not copy full article text from external sources.

## File Naming Rules

Use lowercase, descriptive, hyphenated filenames.

Recommended paths:

- `assets/images/founder-vishal.jpg`
- `assets/images/nova-sc-dashboard.png`
- `assets/images/nova-b1-board.png`
- `assets/images/nova-architecture.png`
- `assets/images/medis-concept.png`
- `assets/images/vesperus-logo-mark.svg`
- `assets/references/aivancity-article.md`
- `assets/references/links.md`

Avoid spaces, vague filenames, version suffixes, and source-camera filenames.
