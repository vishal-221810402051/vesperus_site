# Animation Guide

## Motion Principle

Animation for Vesperus should be subtle, precise, and credibility-building.

Motion should support a premium deep-tech experience without making the website feel childish, theatrical, or medically exaggerated.

## Subtle Page Load Animation

- Fade primary page content in gently.
- Use low vertical movement, such as `8px` to `16px`.
- Keep duration short, around `400ms` to `700ms`.
- Avoid staged sequences that delay access to content.

## Hero Glow Animation

- Use a slow, restrained cyan or teal glow in the hero background.
- Violet may appear as a secondary low-opacity highlight.
- Keep opacity low and movement minimal.
- The glow should never obscure text or imply active medical synthesis.

## Card Hover Lift

- Cards may lift by `2px` to `4px` on hover.
- Border color may shift subtly toward cyan or teal.
- Shadow may increase slightly.
- The hover effect should feel responsive, not floaty.

## Scroll Reveal

- Use scroll reveal for major sections and grouped content.
- Use short duration and low movement.
- Reveal content once, without repetitive effects.
- Keep text readable if animations are disabled or delayed.

## Reduced-Motion Support

- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.
- Disable non-essential movement for reduced-motion users.
- Preserve opacity or instant state changes where needed.
- Do not make important content dependent on animation.

## No Aggressive Animation

Do not use:

- Fast flicker
- Bouncy transitions
- Heavy parallax
- Overactive particles
- Spinning medical or technology graphics
- Large animated orb-heavy backgrounds
- Effects that imply clinical deployment, active treatment, enabled actuator control, or medicine synthesis
